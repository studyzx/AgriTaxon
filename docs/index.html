Overview

Correctly naming a species from its image is the starting point for intelligent agriculture—from choosing a pesticide to enforcing quarantine. AgriTaxon tests whether Large Multimodal Models can perform this task in the open world, without predefined label sets.

Key Findings

  • The best model reaches 83% on multiple-choice but drops to 33% when naming species freely—a seeing-without-naming gap.
  • Pest naming accuracy falls as low as 4% under open-ended evaluation.
  • Models perform 2× better on popular species than on obscure ones.
  • An agentic cropping tool more than doubles accuracy by letting models zoom into diagnostic details.
